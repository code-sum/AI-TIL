- Introduction
  - Self-supervised Learning in Audio-Visual Modality
    - Contrastive Learning (CL)
      - Learns by comparing samples. It pulls representations of paired audio and video closer, while pushing unpaired samples apart.
      - Excellent at learning the correspondence between modalities, creating a Coordinated Representation ideal for retrieval tasks.
        - coordinated representation: process unimodal signals separately, but enforce certain similarity constraints on them
    - Masked Data Modeling (MDM)
      - Learns by reconstructing missing parts. It randomly masks a large portion of the input data and tries to predict the masked content.
