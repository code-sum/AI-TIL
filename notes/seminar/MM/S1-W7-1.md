### [ICLR 2025] Reducing Hallucinations in Large Vision Language Models via Latent Space Steering [🗂️](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.15778)

- 3줄 요약
  - LVLM 의 환각 현상은 별도로 학습된 이미지 인코더와 텍스트 디코더 사이의 불일치 및 시각 입력에 대한 디코더의 민감성 때문에 발생
  - 이를 해결하기 위해 추론 과정에서 잠재 공간의 표현을 조정하여 시각적 특징의 안정성을 높이는 VTI 기술을 제안
  - VTI는 추가 비용 없이 모든 작업에 적용 가능한 범용 기술로, 실험 결과 기존 방식보다 환각을 효과적으로 줄이고 모델의 신뢰도를 높임

- Large Vision Language Models (LVLMs)
  - Take (Image, Texual Instructions) as input,
  - Generate Context-aware Language Responses
    - Image Captioning
    - Visual Question Answering + Instruction Following

- Limitations in LVLMs
  - Hallucinations in LVLMs
    - Generating inaccurate descriptions, or incorrect spatial relations

- Prior Works & Motivation
  - Prior Works
    - Analyzing Hallucinations in LVLMs
      - [2,3] over-reliance on statistical pre-training bias and language prior
      - Results in "biased" feature learning
    - Behavior Control in LLMs
      - [5] Representation Steering
      - where LLMs' behavior is altered by editing latent features (+) Instruction Following, Safety and Harmless
  - Research Goal
    - (1) Investigate hallucinations w.r.t Unstable Information flow from $V_{enc}$ to $T_{dec}$
    - (2) Reduce Hallucinations via Representation Steering link LLM cases

- Investigating Hallucination
  - Explore Correlations: Vision Stability ↔ Hallucinations
    - Key idea #1: Averaging Features of Noise-Added Images
      - (+) More Stable, Robust Representations of Image
        - i.e.) Avg #perturbations increase → Hallucination Reduces
      - (-) Perturbing leads to Loss of Details
      - (-) Multiple forward Passes

- Method: VTI
  - VTI: Visual and Textual Intervention
    - Key idea #2: Precompute & Apply "Direction" of more stable features
      - (1) Visual Stable Direction Vector
      - (2) PCA
    - Pushing the Latent Features to
      - (1) Stable Direction to Image Perturbation
      - (2) Non-Hallucination Direction
    - (1) Shifting Visual Features: $h_{l,t}^{v} := h_{l,t}^{v} + \alpha \cdot d_{l,t}^{\text{vision}}$
    - (2) Shifting Textual Features: $h_{l,t}^{x,v} := h_{l,t}^{x,v} + \beta \cdot d_{l,t=\text{last token}}^{\text{text}}$
